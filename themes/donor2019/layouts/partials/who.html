Compared to 2018, commitments from bilateral financing partners in 2019 jumped by 25%, totaling $3.67 billion. In contrast, counting of commitments from multilateral financing partners was affected by the new and stricter definition of cofinancing. Some $1.97 billion cofinancing from the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank for three projects were not included in 2019’s report of commitments, reducing the 2019 commitment to $905 million, down by 71% from 2018’s total.

ADB’s top 3 financing partners in terms of cofinancing commitments─Japan, Germany, and the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development─ each committed over $300 million. Collectively, they account for 73% of sovereign cofinancing for 2019. In terms of numbers of projects cofinanced, Japan, Korea, and Australia led the group with 29, 21, and 19 projects, respectively.

The biggest loan cofinancing for 2019 was with the Japan International Cooperation Agency for the Malolos-Clark Railway Project in the Philippines, up to $2.1 billion of the project’s $2.75 billion budget.

Over 92% of cofinancing were for loan projects, totaling $4.52 billion. In terms of volume, the top three partners were Japan, Germany, and European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD), accounting for 79% of loan cofinancing. Almost half (45%) of sovereign loan cofinancing can be attributed to JICA’s commitment for the Malolos-Clark Railway Project.

In terms of number of loans cofinanced, Germany had 3 projects while Japan, Korea, France, EBRD, and the Climate Investment Fund had 2 projects each.
